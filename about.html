Chapter One

The City Nobody Talks About

There is a version of this story that starts with a garage in Silicon Valley, a Stanford degree, and a $2 million seed round. This is not that story. This story starts on the streets of Savannah, Georgia.

Tourists come for the cobblestone squares, the Spanish moss, the pastel row houses and ghost tours. They take their pictures and leave. What they don't see are the people living in the in-between spaces of that beautiful city — the ones who know which doorways offer shelter when Georgia rain comes. Michael Meier Jr. knows those spaces. He has lived in them for four years.

"Not couch-surfing homeless. Not between-apartments homeless. The real kind — the sustained kind that doesn't feel cinematic. It feels like evidence that maybe the people who doubted you were right."

Four years without a permanent address. Four years of waking up and deciding — against every signal the world was sending — that this was not the end of the story.

Chapter Two

The Decision Nobody Saw

While most people with internet access were using their phones to scroll, Michael was using his to study. Not casually. With the focused energy of a person who has correctly identified that the window is open — and understands that windows close.

He taught himself AI architecture, SaaS business models, API integrations, database design, and revenue strategy — entirely on a Moto G Android phone. No laptop. No office. No team. Just a man, a signal, and a decision.

Every barrier that might cause a person with resources to slow down — the technical complexity, the financial uncertainty, the sheer audacity of believing you can build a technology company from nothing — he encountered those barriers without the safety net that makes most people willing to try. He tried anyway.

Chapter Three

Why He Built Free Will AI

Michael built Free Will AI because he understood something deeply personal about the gap between people who have resources and people who don't: people without resources can't afford to trade time for small returns anymore. They need systems. They need leverage. They need something that works while they sleep.

He built the tool he needed himself. Then he decided to give it to everyone.

"Financial freedom for my family. Prove everyone who doubted me wrong. Build something that lasts. Change my life completely. Not four goals — one fire burning in four directions at once."
Chapter Four

What the Name Means

"Free Will AI" is not a marketing decision. It is a philosophical statement from a man who knows what it means to have your circumstances try to override your choices. Free will — the actual concept that you are not controlled by your situation — was not abstract to Michael. It was his daily practice of choosing to build when everything around him pointed toward surrender.

The product is the proof. The existence of Free Will AI is the argument. He did not wait until his circumstances were comfortable enough to deserve a dream. He started in the worst conditions available — and he built something real.
